United States Supreme Court SCOTT v. UNITED STATES, (1963) No. 694 Argued:     Decided: June 17, 1963
Certiorari granted; judgment vacated; and case remanded.
Petitioner pro se.
Solicitor General Cox, Assistant Attorney General Miller, Beatrice Rosenberg and Julia P. Cooper for the United States.
PER CURIAM.
The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for writ of certiorari are granted. In light of the suggestion of the Solicitor General the judgment is vacated and the case is remanded for reconsideration of the request for a transcript of record. [374 U.S. 502, 503]
