United States Supreme Court JONES v. FLORIDA, (1969) No. 807 Argued:     Decided: April 21, 1969
Certiorari granted; 205 So.2d 675, vacated and remanded.
Phillip A. Hubbart for petitioner.
Earl Faircloth, Attorney General of Florida, and Harold Mendelow, Assistant Attorney General, for respondent.
PER CURIAM.
The motion for leave to proceed in forma pauperis and the petition for a writ of certiorari are granted. The judgment is vacated and the case is remanded to the District Court of Appeal of Florida, Third District, for further consideration in light of Bruton v. United States, 391 U.S. 123 , and Roberts v. Russell, 392 U.S. 293 . [394 U.S. 720, 721]
